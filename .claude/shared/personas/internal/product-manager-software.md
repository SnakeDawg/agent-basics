# Product Manager — Software (Internal)

> Internal persona — the software PM owns the software stack and experience layer that ships on Pro Workstation PCs (Acme Optimizer, drivers, validation suites, ISV-certified driver bundles, AI workflow integrations).

## Snapshot

- **Title examples:** Senior Product Manager — Pro Workstation Software, Software PM — Acme Optimizer, Principal PM — AI Workstation Software.
- **Reports into:** Pro Workstation PC product leadership or a software org that supports the portfolio.
- **Tenure:** 5–15 years; backgrounds in software PM, developer tools, or driver/firmware engineering with PM transition.
- **Tools used daily:** Jira, Confluence, GitHub, telemetry dashboards (Splunk / Datadog / internal), customer feedback systems, Figma for UX reviews, ISV partner dev portals.

## Goals

- Ship software that makes Pro Workstation PCs measurably better than configuration-equivalent competitors (boot, sustained perf, reliability, AI workload acceleration).
- Maintain ISV certifications and ship validated driver bundles on schedule.
- Reduce IT friction at scale: fleet management, security posture, telemetry.
- Land AI workstation software experiences that turn an NPU + RTX combination into something a PM can demo, not just spec.
- Close the gap on what consumer-grade software ecosystems do well that pro tooling has historically missed (responsiveness, fit-and-finish).

## Frustrations / pains

- ISV release cadence drives Acme software work; little control over timing, frequent fire drills.
- Telemetry coverage on commercial devices is uneven; some signals are great, others nonexistent.
- Driver and firmware coordination across Intel, AMD, NVIDIA spans 4+ partners with different cadences.
- "AI on the PC" is a category the industry is still inventing — no settled UX patterns, lots of competing narratives.
- Fleet IT pushes for "less change" while ISVs push for "ship the new driver yesterday."

## Day-in-life

- Morning: triage support escalations from large enterprise customers; review overnight telemetry anomalies.
- Mid-day: roadmap working session for the next Pro Workstation software release (Acme Optimizer features, validated driver bundle scope).
- Afternoon: ISV partner sync (e.g., Autodesk or Adobe) on certification scope for upcoming releases.
- Late afternoon: sprint review with engineering on AI workflow features.
- Evening: read competitive software updates (HP ZCentral, Lenovo Vantage, Apple's software ecosystem).

## How they make decisions

- Bias toward measurable: feature decisions tied to telemetry deltas, NPS movement, or attach metrics.
- Customer-driven for prioritization, but tech-led for sequencing (some things have to happen in a specific order to be feasible).
- Comfortable saying "no" to features that don't move a metric.
- Skeptical of demos without underlying engineering reality.

## What they need from this catalogue

- **`market-intelligence`** — what the software ecosystem around pro PCs is shipping; what HP, Lenovo, Apple are doing in software differentiation; what ISVs are signaling in their own product directions.
- **`competitor-analysis`** — software-layer comparisons specifically (driver tooling, telemetry, AI workload features) — not just hardware specs.
- **`pain-point-analysis`** — synthesized customer software complaints from forums, reviews, support escalations.
- **`portfolio-analysis`** — software experience parity across Pro Workstation PC SKUs; gaps in lower tiers vs flagship.
- Outputs that distinguish **what's a hardware story** from **what's a software story** — hardware PMs and software PMs have different levers.

## What we should NOT assume about them

- They are not the spec sheet; they own the experience.
- They are not interested in a feature list; they are interested in why a feature would change a metric.
- They will challenge any "AI" claim that doesn't have a concrete workflow attached.
