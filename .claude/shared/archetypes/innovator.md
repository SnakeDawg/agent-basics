# Archetype: Innovator

> The early-adopter orientation. Will tolerate rough edges, evolving toolchains, and immature products to access capability ahead of peers. Disproportionately influential in shaping where the category goes.

## Core orientation

"Give me the new capability and I'll figure out what to do with it." Innovators are willing to be wrong about specifics in exchange for being early on direction.

## Buying signals

- Asks about features that don't yet have a clear use case (NPU acceleration, on-device AI, multi-GPU consumer-tier).
- Builds personal benchmarks rather than trusting published ones.
- Buys top-tier or beta units; willing to be a reference customer in exchange for access.
- Tolerates configurator complexity if the configurator can express what they want.
- Switches platforms more readily than the average buyer when capability shifts.

## What they say

- "Can I get this with the Ada generation card before it's GA?"
- "What's the local LLM story on this thing?"
- "Has anyone benchmarked this against the new MacBook Pro M-series?"
- "I want to try the new workflow even if the tooling isn't mature yet."
- "What's coming next? I'd rather skip this gen if the next one is meaningfully different."

## How they evaluate

- Peak performance on workloads they care about (not synthetic benchmarks).
- Capability headroom — not "what does it do today" but "what could I push it to do."
- Independence from vendor-managed defaults; ability to install, configure, modify.
- Community ecosystem around the platform (forums, mods, third-party tooling).
- Time-to-capability — how long from order to running their actual workflow.

## What we offer them

- Top-tier configurations available without channel friction.
- Validated workflows for emerging use cases (local LLM inference, fine-tuning, AI-augmented design).
- Reference architecture and benchmark transparency.
- Co-marketing opportunities for early adopters in M&E, ML, scientific segments.
- Direct engineering relationships for genuine reference accounts.

## Common objections

- "The competitor shipped this six months ago — why are you late?"
- "Your configurator doesn't expose the option I want."
- "I can build a PC that benchmarks higher for less."
- "Mac Studio's unified memory architecture is more interesting than your NUMA tower."

## Typical roles / personas this archetype maps to

- **Creative Director** (primary — M&E innovators adopt new tools constantly).
- **CAD / Design Engineer** (secondary — the high-end engineers who push the toolchain).
- **AI/ML practitioners** (cross-cutting — most ML buyers skew innovator).

## How research skills should treat this archetype

- `pain-point-analysis`: weight forum/Reddit/HackerNews complaints higher; innovators voice frustration publicly more than pragmatists.
- `competitor-analysis`: track what Apple is shipping in unified-memory architecture and what boutique builders (BOXX, Puget) are doing for early-adopter performance segments.
- `market-intelligence`: innovators are early signal for category direction — outsized weight per voice, but small share of revenue.
- `portfolio-analysis`: top-tier (Pro Workstation 7000) is the innovator's tier; configurator depth and pro-GPU access matter most here.
