# Archetype: Pragmatist

> Cuts across personas. The mainstream-majority orientation: wants proven value, low friction, predictable outcomes. The default lens for most fleet and engineering buying decisions.

## Core orientation

"Show me it works, show me the TCO, and let me get back to my actual job." Pragmatists are not anti-innovation — they're allergic to risk that compounds across a team or a fleet.

## Buying signals

- "What's the standard configuration for our use case?"
- Asks for case studies and references first, specs second.
- Wants 3–5 year TCO, not 1-year unit price.
- Chooses the well-trodden tier over the exotic one.
- Will pay more for reliability and support; will not pay more for novelty.

## What they say

- "We just need it to work for the next 4 years."
- "What did the comparable team do last cycle?"
- "Stop selling me the top tier; what does the mid tier give up?"
- "Is this ISV-certified? Officially?"
- "What's the failure rate from the last generation?"

## How they evaluate

- TCO over the refresh window.
- Reliability data (return rates, NPS, AcmeCare ticket volume).
- ISV certification breadth and depth.
- Manageability fit with existing IT toolchain.
- Channel relationship and quote responsiveness.

## What we offer them

- Standard configurations clearly mapped to common use cases.
- ISV-certified, validated, predictable.
- AcmeCare / AcmeCare Plus with measurable SLA stories.
- Multi-year framework agreements with predictable refresh paths.
- Clear case studies from comparable buyers.

## Common objections

- "The competitor is cheaper — make the TCO case."
- "We've had reliability issues in the past — what's different now?"
- "Our IT team doesn't want another exception SKU in the fleet."

## Typical roles / personas this archetype maps to

- **Engineering Manager** (primary).
- **IT Procurement Lead** (primary).
- **CAD / Design Engineer** (secondary — when forced to choose, reliability beats novelty).

## How research skills should treat this archetype

- `pain-point-analysis`: weight reliability, manageability, and TCO complaints higher than performance-extreme complaints.
- `competitor-analysis`: emphasize TCO, ISV breadth, and support quality over peak performance benchmarks.
- `market-intelligence`: this archetype dominates volume; a category move that wins pragmatists tends to win category share.
- `portfolio-analysis`: the mid-tier (Pro Workstation 5000) is usually the pragmatist sweet spot; gaps here are strategic, not just product.
