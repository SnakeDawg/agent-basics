# Pro Workstation — Portfolio Strategy

> How Pro Workstation wins as a portfolio. Constrained by CDG Commercial strategy; constrains the Pro Workstation PC product strategy.

## Strategic vision (3-year)

Establish Pro Workstation as the default workstation choice for AI-augmented professional workflows — the platform where CAD, simulation, content creation, and on-device AI inference all converge.

## Where we play

- **Customer segments:**
  - **Engineering / Manufacturing** — CAD, CAE, CAM, PLM workflows.
  - **AEC** (Architecture, Engineering, Construction) — Revit, ArchiCAD, BIM, large drawing sets.
  - **Media & Entertainment** — video editing, 3D animation, VFX, color grading, audio.
  - **Scientific / Research** — data analysis, GIS, lab data, ML model development.
  - **Financial Services** — multi-monitor trading, quantitative modeling, risk simulation.
  - **Healthcare** — medical imaging, PACS, genomics workstations.
- **Geographies:** Global, with strongest position in North America manufacturing/AEC and EMEA engineering verticals.
- **Form factors:** Mobile (laptops 14–17"), Desktop (compact, tower), Rack (centralized).

## How we win

1. **ISV partnership depth.** Pro Workstation is independently certified across the broadest set of professional ISVs in the industry — competitors must match this on every refresh.
2. **NVIDIA RTX professional GPU lineup.** Pro-grade drivers, ECC memory, multi-app stability that consumer GeForce cards cannot match for production use.
3. **Configurability advantage.** Tower workstations remain fully serviceable and upgradable in an industry trending toward sealed designs.
4. **Reliability under sustained load.** Thermal design, component selection, and burn-in testing tuned for 8–12 hour rendering, simulation, and inference workloads.
5. **AI workstation positioning.** Pro Workstation as the on-ramp for professionals running local LLMs, fine-tuning models, or doing AI-augmented design — bridging individual workstation use and IDG AI infrastructure.

## Strategic priorities (next 12 months)

1. **Mobile workstation share gain** — close gap against Lenovo ThinkPad P-series; emphasize thin-and-light pro segment (Pro Workstation 5000 series).
2. **AI workstation narrative** — establish Pro Workstation as the "AI-ready" pro device; partner with NVIDIA, ISVs on validated AI workflows.
3. **ISV refresh cadence** — maintain certifications through the 14th-gen Intel / Core Ultra and AMD Ryzen Pro refresh; add new ISVs in scientific/ML segment.
4. **Margin defense** — premium tier mix, services attach (AcmeCare Plus, AcmeDeploy for Pro Workstation specifically).
5. **Sustainability scorecard** — recycled aluminum chassis, recycled materials in packaging — increasingly important in EMEA RFPs.

## Success metrics

- Workstation segment market share (per IDC / Gartner workstation tracker) overall and by form factor.
- Mobile workstation share specifically (the contested segment vs Lenovo).
- Premium SKU mix (Pro Workstation 7000 series) as proxy for margin.
- ISV certification count and ISV co-marketing engagements.
- AcmeCare Plus attach rate (Pro Workstation-specific).
- NPS within the workstation buyer segment.

## Time horizons

- **Now (0–6 mo):** Execute current refresh; defend share in core engineering and AEC accounts; respond to Lenovo P-series moves.
- **Next (6–18 mo):** AI workstation category launch and validation; deeper ML/data science ISV certifications; mobile workstation thin-and-light positioning.
- **Future (18 mo+):** New form factors for AI workloads (multi-GPU compact desktops, Pro Workstation tower with AI-specific options); deeper integration with IDG AI infrastructure as a hybrid story.

## Key competitors

- **HP Z** — closest peer; strong in M&E and creative; ZBook Studio is a notable mobile competitor.
- **Lenovo ThinkStation + ThinkPad P-series** — strongest in mobile workstation segment; very competitive in AEC and engineering.
- **Apple Mac Studio / Mac Pro / MacBook Pro M-series** — formidable in M&E and software development; less relevant in CAD/CAE due to ISV gaps.
- **Microsoft Surface for Business** — niche; no real workstation tier but creeps into creative segments.
- **Custom builders / boutique workstation vendors** (BOXX, Puget, etc.) — niche but high-credibility in performance segments.

## Key risks

- Lenovo P-series mobile share momentum.
- Apple ecosystem expansion into traditional Pro Workstation customer bases (especially M&E).
- Workstation TAM softening if AI inference moves to cloud rather than on-device.
- ISV consolidation reducing differentiation if certifications become table stakes.
- NVIDIA pro-GPU supply constraints affecting refresh cadence.
