# Commercial Devices Group — Commercial — Charter

> Scope, authority, decision rights, and stakeholders for the commercial sub-segment of CDG.

## In scope

- The **Acme Standard**, **Acme Plus**, and **Pro Workstation** portfolios. Positioning and SKU detail live in the portfolio docs.
- **Pro Workstation** — commercial workstations (mobile and desktop; the focus of this catalogue's product-level work).
- Commercial displays, monitors, docks, peripherals attached to the commercial portfolio.
- AcmeCare, AcmeDeploy, and Acme Pay PCaaS attach motions tied to commercial devices.
- Commercial channel programs and direct sales motion for commercial accounts.

## Out of scope

- **Acme Home (consumer), Acme Gaming (gaming), and the broader Acme consumer line** — owned by CDG Consumer.
- **IDG portfolio** (servers, storage, networking, HCI, data protection) — different segment leadership.
- **Software-only products and standalone services** unattached to commercial client devices.

## Decision rights

| Decision | Owner |
| --- | --- |
| CDG Commercial P&L | CDG Commercial President |
| Portfolio strategy (Acme Standard, Acme Plus, Pro Workstation) | Portfolio GMs reporting to CDG Commercial President |
| Pricing approval (above threshold) | Commercial finance + portfolio GM |
| Major customer escalations | Account team + commercial leadership |
| Channel program design | Channel chief + CDG Commercial leadership |
| Roadmap prioritization | Portfolio GMs + CDG Commercial CTO |

## Key stakeholders

- **Customers:** IT decision-makers, procurement, end users in commercial accounts.
- **Channel:** Tier-1 distributors, regional resellers, MSPs, system integrators, public-sector contracting partners.
- **Partners:** Microsoft (Windows, Copilot, Surface coopetition), Intel, AMD, NVIDIA, ISVs (Autodesk, Adobe, Dassault, Siemens, etc.), security vendors.
- **Internal:** IDG (cross-sell), services org (AcmeCare/AcmeDeploy), supply chain, Acme Financial Services, marketing, HR.
- **Regulators:** Public-sector compliance bodies (FedRAMP for some services, CMMC for defense, GDPR/data residency in EMEA).

## Operating cadence

- Monthly operating reviews per portfolio (Acme Standard, Acme Plus, Pro Workstation).
- Quarterly business reviews with channel partners.
- Quarterly product roadmap reviews against ISV and platform partner roadmaps.
- Annual commercial AOP (annual operating plan) refresh; mid-year update.

## Success metrics (segment-level)

- Revenue and unit volume by portfolio.
- Market share movement by portfolio (vs HP, Lenovo, Apple, ASUS commercial).
- Services attach rate (AcmeCare, AcmeDeploy, Acme Pay).
- Premium-tier mix (margin proxy).
- Channel partner satisfaction and tier-2/3 partner engagement.
- Customer NPS, IT decision-maker preference.
