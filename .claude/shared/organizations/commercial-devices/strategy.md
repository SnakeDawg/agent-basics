# Commercial Devices Group — Commercial — Strategy

> How CDG Commercial wins. Constrained by Acme Computing corporate strategy; constrains the Pro Workstation, Acme Standard, and Acme Plus portfolio strategies.

## Strategic vision (3-year)

Lead the commercial PC category through the AI PC refresh cycle, capture share in workstations, and extend services attach so commercial PCs become a multi-year revenue stream rather than a transactional sale.

## Where we play

- **Customer segments:** Large Enterprise, Mid-Market, Small Business, Public Sector (Federal, SLED), Healthcare, Financial Services, Manufacturing/AEC, Media & Entertainment, Energy.
- **Geographies:** Global, with go-to-market motion split between direct (top accounts) and channel (mid-market, SMB, public sector).
- **Form factors:** Commercial laptops, desktops, mobile and tower workstations, thin clients, displays, peripherals, and docks.

## How we win

1. **Manageability + security as default.** Acme Device Manager, Trusted Device, SafeBIOS, vPro integration. IT-friendly out of the box.
2. **ISV certifications and validation.** Especially for Pro Workstation — measurable performance against Autodesk, Dassault, Siemens, Adobe, ANSYS, Bentley, PTC workloads.
3. **Lifecycle services attach.** AcmeCare (reactive), AcmeDeploy (deployment), Acme Pay PC-as-a-Service (consumption). Each unit shipped is the start of a multi-year revenue relationship.
4. **AI PC narrative.** Leverage NPU + Copilot+ momentum to drive refresh urgency, especially in mid-market and SMB.
5. **Sustainable design.** Recycled content, modular components, energy efficiency — increasingly a procurement criterion for enterprise and public sector.

## Strategic priorities (next 12 months)

1. **Capture AI PC refresh** — drive refresh urgency in the installed base; pair AI PC narrative with security and manageability story.
2. **Workstation share capture** — close the gap with HP Z and Lenovo ThinkStation; expand mobile workstation share.
3. **Services attach growth** — increase AcmeCare + AcmeDeploy attach rates; grow Acme Pay PCaaS pipeline.
4. **Channel enablement** — deepen partner programs in mid-market and SMB; reduce time-to-quote.
5. **Sustainability scorecard** — meet 2030 commitments; respond to growing customer RFP requirements.

## Success metrics

- Commercial PC unit volume and revenue.
- Market share by category — commercial notebook and desktop, plus workstation (mobile and tower).
- Services attach rate (AcmeCare, AcmeDeploy, Acme Pay).
- Mix of premium SKUs (margin proxy).
- Customer NPS and IT decision-maker preference scores.
- Channel partner satisfaction.

## Time horizons

- **Now (0–6 mo):** Drive AI PC refresh; defend share in core commercial accounts; execute services attach playbook.
- **Next (6–18 mo):** Workstation share gain; Acme Pay PCaaS scaling; sustainability differentiation in EMEA RFPs.
- **Future (18 mo+):** New form factors (foldables, dual-screen, on-device AI workloads); deeper integration with Microsoft Copilot ecosystem; potential category expansion (e.g., AI workstations as a sub-tier).

## Key risks

- HP and Lenovo aggressive pricing in commercial.
- Apple making deeper enterprise inroads (especially M&E, design segments where Pro Workstation plays).
- AI PC adoption plateauing if Copilot+ value isn't material.
- Component cost spikes (memory, NPU silicon).
